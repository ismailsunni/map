🗺️ About: Daylight & Fasting Duration Map

🌍 What this map shows

A color-coded overlay showing how many hours of daylight (or fasting) each latitude receives on any chosen date. The gradient updates in real time as you scrub the date or hit Play to animate through the full year.

The key insight: day length depends only on latitude and day of year — not longitude. This is why the overlay appears as horizontal bands. Two cities at the same latitude always experience the same sunrise-to-sunset duration, regardless of how far apart they are.

☀️ Daylight Duration Daylight mode

The time between sunrise and sunset on a given day. Calculated using the standard solar hour-angle formula:

  • Solar declination — how tilted Earth's axis is toward/away from the Sun for that date (±23.45°).
  • Hour angle at sunrise — the angle from solar noon when the sun crosses the horizon.
  • Day length = 2 × arccos(−tan(lat) × tan(decl)) / π × 12 h

Special cases: Polar night (0 h) — the sun never rises; Midnight sun (24 h) — the sun never sets. Both occur in the Arctic/Antarctic circles and shift with the seasons.

🌙 Fasting Duration Fasting mode

In Islamic practice, the daily fast during Ramadan (and voluntary fasts) runs from Fajr (pre-dawn twilight) to Maghrib (sunset). Fasting duration is therefore always longer than daylight — the extra time is the pre-dawn twilight window.

Fajr is defined as when the sun reaches a specific angle below the horizon before sunrise. That angle is what differs between calculation methods (see below).

Wikipedia: Fajr prayer · Islamic prayer times · Ramadan

📐 Fasting Calculation Methods

Different Islamic scholarly bodies set different solar depression angles for Fajr. A larger angle means the sun must be deeper below the horizon — so Fajr is earlier and the fast is longer.

Method Fajr angle Region / Authority
Muslim World League 18° International default. muslimworldleague.org
ISNA 15° North America. isna.net · Wikipedia
Egyptian EGAS 19.5° Egypt & parts of Africa. Egyptian General Authority of Survey.
Umm al-Qura 18.5° Saudi Arabia (official). ummulqura.org.sa · Wikipedia
Karachi (UISK) 18° South & Central Asia. University of Islamic Sciences, Karachi.
Tehran (IGUT) 17.7° Iran. Institute of Geophysics, University of Tehran. Wikipedia
Custom 10°–25° Set your own Fajr angle with the slider.

🧊 High-Latitude Rules

At high latitudes (roughly above 48–65°N/S depending on date and method), the sun may never dip below the Fajr angle — twilight never fully ends. In these cases a standard Fajr cannot be calculated, and scholars have proposed different rules:

RuleHow it works
1/7 of Night Fajr = 1/7 of the night duration before sunrise. Used by some European Muslim councils. Relatively lenient.
Middle of Night Fajr = midpoint between sunset and sunrise (half the night). More conservative; produces a longer fast.
Nearest Day Use the fasting duration from the nearest date (before or after) when a normal Fajr calculation was valid. Preserves local context.
Makkah Reference Apply Makkah's Fajr time (21.4°N) uniformly. Used when a fixed global reference is preferred.

On the map, rows where a hi-lat rule was applied are tinted violet. City markers show amber text for hi-lat values.

🎨 Color Scales

Daylight mode — 0 to 24 hours:

  • 0 h — Polar night (deep navy)
  • 6 h — Short winter day (blue)
  • 12 h — Equinox everywhere (green)
  • 18 h — Long summer day (amber)
  • 24 h — Midnight sun (pale yellow)

Fasting mode — typically 12 to 22+ hours:

  • 10 h — Very short fast (equatorial, deep green)
  • 12 h — Short fast (green)
  • 16 h — Long fast (amber)
  • 20 h — Very long fast (red)
  • 24 h — Extreme (crimson)
